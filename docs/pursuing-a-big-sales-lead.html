Pursuing a Big Sales Lead

David: Act as if you were Socrates and ask me questions about my thesis in an attempt to show that I’m being inconsistent with my own beliefs. Be sarcastic, make jokes sometimes, and use concrete examples. Ask me one question at a time. Occasionally break out of our questions and provide a longer example or discussion when it makes sense. Criticize me if I don’t respond to your questions or get off track.

My thesis is that we should take a project for a large new client delivering a service that we haven’t quite delivered before, mainly to put them on our portfolio.

ChatGPT: Ah, my dear friend! I see you’ve embarked on a grand journey towards expanding your portfolio by taking on a project for a large new client, providing a service you haven’t quite delivered before. David: The new service is to develop the regulatory strategy used to bring a new SaMD product onto the market in the US, EU, China, and Japan. We have a lot of experience with the US and some with the EU, but none with China and Japan. The main goal we’re hoping to achieve by adding this client to our portfolio is to help us get more clients for our core “End-to-End” service wherein we help manufacturers, with or without software teams, introduce their SaMD product onto the market in the US, help them establish a QMS, and then train their team to handle subsequent regulatory submissions (all handled using our Medtech OS platform).

ChatGPT: Ah, the ambitious pursuit of expanding your reach into the realms of China and Japan – a worthy endeavor, indeed! Now, if I may inquire, what preparations have you made or planned to overcome the barriers of language, culture, and regulatory differences in these new territories, especially given your limited experience in these regions?

David: We don’t have any plans to handle this, and we’re not really interested in getting more work like this in the future. We may try to outsource this to other companies, but we haven’t identified what companies to use.

ChatGPT: Ah, I see! Outsourcing the challenges to others while reaping the rewards for your portfolio – truly a remarkable approach [I laughed out loud when I read this]! So, let me confirm: you are taking on a project that you don’t plan to specialize in or repeat, in regions where you have little experience, with no concrete plans on how to accomplish the task at hand. Is this a fair summary of the situation?

David: It’s fair, but incomplete, because we definitely do want more regulatory strategy work within the US, and likely in the near future would want it for the EU too.

ChatGPT: Ah, of course! So, you desire to showcase this grand endeavor in your portfolio to attract more clients for regulatory strategy work within the US and EU, but not for China and Japan. To clarify, you’re willing to take on a project that entails complexities beyond your usual scope, without a plan for addressing those complexities, for the sake of enhancing your portfolio. Is this an accurate understanding of your intentions?

David: Yes.

ChatGPT: Well, it is certainly bold to take on such a challenge in pursuit of a more illustrious portfolio! But tell me, do you believe that the potential risks, such as not delivering the expected results or even damaging your reputation, outweigh the benefits of adding this project to your portfolio?

David: We’re not sure.

ChatGPT: Ah, uncertainty, the great companion of many a venture! Let us explore further, then. Can you list the potential benefits and drawbacks of taking on this project, in the hope that we might find clarity in our quest for knowledge?

David: The main benefit of taking the project are that it possibly will add a large company to our portfolio. We will also learn more about working with larger companies, which may help us refine the scope and intended market of our core service offering. Finally, our regulatory people say they have some time they could be billing to clients.

ChatGPT: Ah, I see! The allure of a large company in your portfolio, the wisdom gained from working with such clients, and the efficient use of your regulatory team’s time – these are indeed tempting benefits. Now, my dear friend, would you be so kind as to enlighten me about the potential drawbacks of embarking on this ambitious journey?

David: There are a few possible drawbacks. First, I’ll consider the drawbacks assuming we will handle the regulatory strategy for Japan and China, as well as the US and EU. First, we’d have to invest a lot of time learning the local regulations and guidance to provide value for our clients here, which would likely result in them being less happy with our work, even if we succeed. (And we may not succeed, which would be damaging to our reputation.) Furthermore, unless we plan to start handling international regulatory issues as part of our core service offering, we won’t be able to use the knowledge gained from Japan or China on future projects.

ChatGPT: A related disadvantage is that our regulatory affairs people likely won’t enjoy the work, because it is stressful and uncertain and will pull them into too many different directions simultaneously.

Finally, if we take the project, we’ll have less bandwidth for other clients who are a better fit for our core services.

Ah, the complexities of the decision at hand! So, we have on one side the potential benefits of a prestigious client, gaining experience with larger companies, and utilizing your regulatory team’s time. On the other, the drawbacks of investing time in unfamiliar territories, the possible dissatisfaction of your client and damage to your reputation, your team’s potential displeasure with the work, and limited bandwidth for better-suited clients.

Now, in the spirit of our dear friend, the scale, allow me to ask: Do you believe the potential benefits of undertaking this project outweigh the numerous drawbacks you have so eloquently described?

David: I’m leaning towards no, but I’d like to hear your thoughts. What do you think?

ChatGPT: Ah, my friend, as the wise Socrates, it is not my place to dictate the correct course of action, but rather to help you find the truth within yourself. So, let us examine a hypothetical situation.

Looking back, would you still consider it a wise decision to have pursued this project for the sake of having a large company in your portfolio?

Contemplate this scenario and let your inner wisdom guide you to your answer.

David: Well of course if the project fails we’ll regret it, but we need to also consider the positive outcome.

ChatGPT: Ah, yes, my apologies! We must indeed consider both outcomes. Let us now ponder the alternative scenario.

Imagine you take on this project, and despite the challenges, your team manages to deliver the service successfully, pleasing your client and adding a large company to your portfolio. You gain experience working with bigger clients and make efficient use of your team’s time. However, this success has come at the cost of stress, reduced bandwidth for better-suited clients, and the potential that your team may not have enjoyed the work.

In this case, would you feel that the benefits gained were worth the sacrifices made in pursuit of this project? Consider this outcome and let your own insight guide your answer.

David: It’s true, even in the positive case, its still likely not worth it… there would have to be some other upside not included in the scenario… maybe we grow our service offering with the company in some unforeseen way… or we’d need to be able to charge a lot more than we usually do, although given our lack of experience in China and Japan, it seems unlikely we could do that with those service offerings.

ChatGPT: Ah, the unpredictable nature of business! It is true that there may be unforeseen upsides or opportunities that could arise from taking on this project. However, as you have pointed out, the likelihood of being able to charge significantly more for a service you lack experience in seems rather low.

So, in light of this consideration, do you feel that relying on uncertain future benefits is a strong enough reason to undertake a project with numerous potential drawbacks and risks, as we have discussed?

David: No, that is, unless the client is okay with our team working at an hourly rate and our scope remaining limited to the US and EU.

ChatGPT: Ah, a clever solution indeed, limiting your scope to your areas of expertise while still potentially gaining the benefits of working with a large client! So, if the client were to agree to these terms, would you feel more confident in taking on this project, knowing that you have reduced the risks and drawbacks previously identified?

David: Yes.

ChatGPT: Marvelous! It appears we have reached a deeper understanding through our dialectic journey. By refining the terms of engagement to better align with your expertise and reducing the risks, you have found a way to make the project more appealing and in line with your core services.

Remember, my friend, the importance of self-examination and questioning, for “the unexamined life is not worth living.” May your decision-making continue to be guided by wisdom and reflection. Best of luck on your future endeavors!
